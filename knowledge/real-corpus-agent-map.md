The sample corpus was too polite. The real corpus shows the word `agent` carrying at least three pressures at once: infrastructure runtime, safety boundary, and species marker.

Directional neighbors made the useful difference. Before-neighbors tend to identify the kind of agent or the domain pulling on it: `classroom`, `air`, `boundary`, `lifecycle`, `autonomous`, `custody`, `ai`. After-neighbors tend to name obligations or actions attached to the term: `approval`, `policy`, `log`, `owner`, `security`, `governance`, `should`, `human`.

That asymmetry is the signal. The word is not only broadening into more domains. It is accumulating duties after the noun. In this corpus, `agent` increasingly means "an actor that needs custody, approval, policy, logs, and recovery boundaries."

## Tool Lesson

The cartographer should keep directional evidence first-class. Combined neighbors can say the word drifted. Before/after neighbors say how it drifted.

The imported corpus should remain generated context, not committed source. The durable artifacts are the map output and this interpretation.
